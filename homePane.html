About me

My name is Federico Ghioldi, and I am a full-time researcher at the Department of Aerospace Science and Technologies at Politecnico di Milano.
Based on what my dear colleagues say, we're also entitled to refer to ourselves as ``Assistant Professors'', even though our unique position (RTD-A, in Italy) remains an enigma beyond our nation's borders. So, it seems I'm that too, among other things.
But, honestly, I see myself more as a Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) developer and a problem-solving enthusiast. Indeed, I find myself face to face with a multitude of challenges each day, and, much to my own surprise, I even manage to figure out a solution to some of them.

Career

To make it simple, I took my BSc in Aerospace Engineering, my M.Sc. in Aeronautical Engineering and my Ph.D. in Aerospace Engineering at Politecnico di Milano. Then, I applied for a RTD-A position at the Department that raised me, and here I am.

About this site

I began developing this website to address a minor issue: I just wanted my business card to include a link to my CV, i.e. a PDF that I could continually update. Things escalated quickly. I experimented with several versions of the website before arriving at its current state. This website's design is inspired by that of Lei Mao, with some modifications to both its features and appearance.
Anyway, here is what you're going to find in this website:

  • My curriculum. [It would have been useless otherwise.]
  • A blog to share my perspective on topics I find interesting (currently WiP).
  • A list of the (side) projects I'm currently carrying out.
  • A way to reach my office or contact me. [Use that info only if absolutely necessary.]
  • A list of frequently asked questions, especially valuable for students in the courses for which I provide assistance.

An important note: I can't stress enough that all opinions of views contained in this website are my own.

Coming soon(er or later)

I plan to use the ``blog'' section as space to store my observations or comments on several topics like CFD, physics, breakthrough technologies, etc. On the other hand, I intend the ``project'' space as a container that will include step-by-step procedures I followed to overcome problems or satisfy personal needs.

Are you sure you need to contact me?

I plan to upload my projects on my personal GitHub (provided they are intended to be open source). Hence, in case of complaints, recommendations or bugs, you can open an issue ticket in the corresponding repository. Similarly, you're going to have the chance to reply below each post in this website. Therefore, the personal information contained in the ``Contact'' tab should only be used when strictly necessary.
